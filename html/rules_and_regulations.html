Basketball Rules and Regulations

Basic Rules

  • Team Composition: 5 players per team on the court at a time with substitutions allowed
  • Game Duration: 4 quarters of 10 minutes each (FIBA rules) with breaks in between
  • Scoring: 2 points for regular field goals, 3 points for shots beyond the arc, 1 point for free throws
  • Starting Play: Jump ball at center court to begin the game
  • Shot Clock: 24 seconds to attempt a shot (FIBA rules)

Court Dimensions

  • Standard court size: 28m × 15m (FIBA)
  • 3-point line: 6.75m from the basket
  • Free-throw line: 4.6m from the backboard

Violations

  • Traveling: Moving without dribbling the ball
  • Double Dribble: Dribbling with both hands or stopping then restarting dribble
  • Backcourt Violation: Returning the ball to backcourt after crossing midcourt
  • 3-Second Violation: Offensive player stays in the key for more than 3 seconds
  • 5-Second Violation: Failure to inbound or advance the ball within 5 seconds
  • 8-Second Violation: Failure to advance the ball past midcourt within 8 seconds
  • 24-Second Violation: Failure to attempt a shot within 24 seconds

Fouls

  • Personal Foul: Illegal physical contact example pushing and holding
  • Technical Foul: Unsportsmanlike conduct by players or coaches
  • Flagrant Foul: Excessive or violent contact
  • Team Fouls: After 5 team fouls per quarter, opponents get free throws

APU Sports Club Specific Rules

  • All players must wear proper basketball attire (jerseys, shorts, basketball shoes)
  • No jewelry or accessories allowed during games
  • Respect referees' decisions - arguing may result in technical fouls
  • Games will be played under FIBA rules unless otherwise specified
  • All participants must be registered APU students or staff
  • Zero tolerance for fighting - automatic ejection and possible suspension

Equipment Regulations

  • Only official size 7 basketballs (for men) or size 6 (for women) allowed in competition
  • Shoes must be non-marking basketball shoes
  • Jerseys must have visible numbers on front and back

Conduct & Sportsmanship

  • All players expected to demonstrate good sportsmanship
  • Verbal abuse of officials, opponents, or spectators will not be tolerated
  • Teams responsible for their supporters' behavior
